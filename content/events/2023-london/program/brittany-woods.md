Through the years, DevOps has remained a staple in how companies drive their technical teams forward to deliver "world-class stability, reliability, availability, and security," as stated in The DevOps Handbook. A key element in how teams can achieve this is through trust and modification to how teams work together. This continued move to DevOps and its reliance on mature and collaborative teams means that culture is critical to an organisation's success in not just their DevOps journey as a whole but in the outcomes they hope to achieve.

Please join me as I outline common misconceptions about the role culture plays in DevOps success and the three houses companies can fall into: that of straw, that of sticks, and that of bricks.
